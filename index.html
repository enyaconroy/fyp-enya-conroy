He was becoming sick and tired of this forest.

Tediously walking in and out and in and out, night after night.

There are only so many different trees to look at.

Although, maybe he was just becoming sick of everything.

He couldn't remember the last time he'd smiled.

He had no reason to.

But hey, he had nothing better to do, so he walked in again, with little to no enthusiasm...
Maybe he could spice things up by taking a different route?

I mean, what's more exciting than spruce trees in a slightly different order?


Oh.
A swing?

Well that could be fun.

If only it wasn't as old and battered as him.
He moved on and rolled his eyes to heaven.

As he did he spotted a group of crows circling overhead.

“Huh, spooky”

He left the sounds of cawing behind him as he continued on.

It's not long before a soft glow in the distance catches his eye.
A beam of moonlight.

With fireflies.

Its almost pretty. Almost.

It’s not enough to slow his pace though.

Several long, slightly less tedious moments pass before
our character hears a low bubbling.

Like a stream?
Or a grove, he realises as he gets closer.

A grove with little glowing mushrooms. And a bubbling brook.

He can’t argue with this one, it IS pretty.
A smile threatens to cross his face.

After waiting and watching he finally continues on his journey.
He observed the letters etched into the tree.

And to his surprise he wasn't disgruntled by them.

In fact he found them quite comforting.

Thinking about the people who must have left them there.

He wondered if they were still happy, still in love, still alive.

He hoped they were.
He walked out with a new found appreciation for the little things.

The beautifully mundane things.

Turns out, if you're not looking hard enough, you really can miss the good amongst the mist.
